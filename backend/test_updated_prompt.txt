The mevalonate-FOXO system is necessary for bisphosphonate-mediated health improvements, involving genes such as farnesyl diphosphate synthetase (FDPS-1) and daf-16 (FOXO) [4]. Knockdown of FDPS-1 (FDPS) prevents zoledronic acid (ZA) mediated lifespan and healthspan extension [5], [14]. Similarly, knockdown of daf-16 (FOXO) decreases survival and movement, with no improvement from ZA co-treatment [5], [14]. The conserved bone-muscle ortholog agxt-2 (BAIBA) is also required for ZA-mediated improvements in survival and movement [5], [14].

Additionally, the C. elegans mitochondrial sirtuin sir-2.3 (SIRT-4) is implicated, as its downregulation might mediate ZA's healthspan effects [1]. Knocking down sir-2.3 increased survival and movement, similar to ZA co-treatment [1]. Bisphosphonates are suggested to act partially through mitochondrial mechanisms, including sirtuin inhibition, to lessen the effects of sarcopenia [12].

Knockdown of igdb-1 (FNDC5) also significantly increased survival and healthspan, with ZA co-treatment displaying similar improvements, suggesting igdb-1 might be downregulated by ZA or acts independently to increase healthspan [1], [10].

Genes found to be dispensable for ZA-induced healthspan include let-756 (FGF21) and sir-2.2 (SIRT-4) [1], [10], [15].
